============================================================
THE FINDING THAT INVALIDATES THE OLD SPEC
============================================================
The EG-IPG graph arms were trained with their message passing switched off, by their own regulariser.

`StageALoss._graph` is an unnormalised sum over EDGES divided only by BATCH SIZE, while every other loss
term is mean-reduced. At ~40k edges/sample the penalty is ~103x the response term; its gradient on the edge
gates is up to ~3.3e+06x the task's; `GRAD_CLIP=1.0` then rescales the whole update by ~1/695. So ~99.98%
of every step drives gates toward zero and the gate dies inside epoch 0. The frozen H1's gate mean is
~1.3e-07 against ~0.61 at init (NOT bit-zero: max 3.54e-07, 0 of 117,174 exactly zero).

Controlled three-arm pilot (`tcell_pipeline.pilot_lambda_graph`, seed 0, 2 epochs), read from artifacts.
All arms share one init (0.678556) — same seed. Gate mean is over all 289,974 edges; neighbourhood
sensitivity is `sensitivity.rel_delta`, measured on a 33,754-edge subsample:

  arm         lambda  loss                       gate mean init -> ep1   dead%   nbhd sens   survived
  baseline    0.01    StageALoss                 0.678556 -> 1.9882e-07  100.0%  9.41e-04    False
  zero        0.0     StageALoss                 0.678556 -> 0.897950      0.7%  9.03e-01    TRUE
  normalised  0.01    EdgeNormalisedStageALoss   0.678556 -> 3.2190e-04   98.2%  4.06e-03    False

Read those three rows carefully:
- With the penalty OFF the gates do not merely survive, they RISE. The prediction task WANTS the edges open.
- The obvious one-line repair FAILS. Per-edge normalisation puts the penalty/response ratio at 0.008 — a
  penalty 400x SMALLER than the task — and the gates still collapse 2,108x. AdamW is scale-invariant per
  parameter, so only the penalty's DIRECTION dominance matters, not its magnitude. This was predicted on
  the record before the run. (One part of that prediction was wrong and session E named it: the synthetic
  said ratio-10 and ratio-1e6 would behave IDENTICALLY, but `normalised` collapses about one epoch slower,
  because the real penalty shrinks as the gates do. Corrected statement: MAGNITUDE SETS THE RATE OF
  COLLAPSE, DIRECTION SETS WHETHER IT HAPPENS. Endpoint predicted right, rate predicted wrong.)

USE `gates.mean`, NOT `sensitivity.gate_mean`. An earlier revision of this file quoted 0.6138 / 2.74e-07 /
0.845 / 2.42e-04 — every one of those is the `sensitivity` sub-block's 33,754-edge subsample, not the arm's
gate mean. One misread field, four wrong numbers, all in the same direction. Corrected 2026-07-21 against
`pilot_{baseline,zero,normalised}.json`. The response figures were never affected (different code path).

WHAT THIS DOES AND DOES NOT LICENSE. It is NOT evidence that the graph helps. It means the experiment did
not test the hypothesis. A redesigned regulariser may re-run and still produce a negative — and that would
then be a REAL negative, which is a perfectly good outcome. Do not read this finding as permission to
expect a positive.

============================================================
THE DECISION THAT GATES EVERYTHING (the user's, not an agent's)
============================================================
How should the graph regulariser be redesigned? This is a research decision about the OBJECTIVE, not a bug
fix, and it cannot be delegated. The options are not equivalent:

  A. NORMALISE PER EDGE AND RE-TUNE lambda. Cheapest. But the pilot shows /|E| alone is insufficient, so
     this means an actual sweep over lambda with the gate mean as a monitored quantity, not one number.
  B. REDESIGN THE PENALTY. e.g. normalise by edge count AND bound the term (a hinge/target-sparsity form
     that cannot dominate once the target is met), or move sparsity into the architecture rather than the
     loss. Most defensible, most work.
  C. DROP L_graph FOR THE HEADLINE FAMILY and report the graph result at lambda=0, with the sparsity term
     recorded as a separate diagnostic. Fastest honest path to a VALID comparison; costs the sparsity claim.
  D. DO NOT RE-RUN. Publish the negative as UNTESTED-BY-CONSTRUCTION: state plainly that the screening
     campaign did not test the graph, retract every graph claim, and stop. Legitimate, and cheap.

Whichever is chosen, the re-screen must reuse the SAME frozen fold, the SAME frozen basis (session C
established that nothing justifies changing it), 5 paired seeds, paired contrasts, and BOTH Bonferroni and
Holm. And the gate mean must be logged per epoch as a first-class quantity, so this cannot recur silently.
